Appears In: Transport Network Management
The Utilisation Management processes facilitate the best possible utilization of the transport network. This means the best possible control of how the transport means operates in the network. The aim is the best possible traffic flow and use of the network resources. Thus, interaction with a lot of different actors and processes are required:<br /></p><p><ul><li>The Logistics Service Client will get traffic and transport network information</li><br /></p><p><li>The Logistics Service Provider will get the same information as the client. In addition traffic management measures will be taken towards the transport means (i.e. the On-board Management process); Transport progress status (TPS) information is provided to the Manage Logistics Operation process.</li><br /></p><p><li>The Logistics Service Provider will also interact with the Network Utilization Manager. Transport means (i.e. the On-board Management process) may provide tracking information, safety status information and entry/exit information for areas. Access and priority may be requested, and transport network resource bookings may be sent form either the Manage Logistics Operation process or the Logistics Unit Control and support process.</li><br /></p><p></ul><br /></p>
